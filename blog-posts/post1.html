Neumorphism in Web Design

12/6/23 7:06PM

Neumorphism relies on subtle shadows and highlights to create the illusion of depth, often making elements appear as if they're extruded from the background or embedded within it. The technique involves using similar background and foreground colors with varying degrees of brightness and shadow, resulting in a tactile, almost interactive interface. Neumorphism uses a minimalist design aesthetic with simple shapes and clean interfaces to maintain a sleek, modern look. Using a minimalist design makes it easier to create the raised or sunken look of the interfaces by simply adding soft shadows and lighting to certain areas of the design. Another telltale sign of neumorphism is the use of monochromatic color gradients, which add to the sleek feel and also makes it easier to create the illusion of depth.

As a relatively new design aesthetic, neumorphism can give a fresh, contemporary look to websites and applications, setting them apart from traditional flat designs. The realistic look of the buttons and interfaces improves the overall user experience by making the entire experience closer to interacting with a physical object, therefore making it more intuitive. While neumorphism does provide a visually appealing look, it also provides some accessibility concerns. The subtle color contrasts and shadows might pose challenges for users with visual impairments, potentially affecting readability and usability. Additionally, the overuse of soft shadows and low-contrast elements might lead to some confusion, making it hard for users to differentiate between interactive and non-interactive elements.

Neumorphism can be found all over the web and in various apps, but it is used most effectively in simple apps that have a lot of buttons and sliders. A good example of this would be a music player, a calculator, or other simple web apps. Neumorphism will be the most successful aesthetic in any website or app that could be made into a physical object, as the entire point of the aesthetic is to make buttons and interfaces feel like they could be physically interacted with.
