My Art

While I am pursuing a career in web design, I have recently fallen in love with creating physical art. Learning about the theory of art in my 2D Design class and the application of those theories in my Basic Drawing class, I have been able to create several works of art that I genuinely feel are good pieces.

On the left, you'll find a collection of the art I have created this year. I have spent over 100 hours this semester alone on these pieces, and they have taught me so much about the rules of design and the creation of good art.

My Journey

At the beginning of this semster, I was not a good artist, but I desperately wanted to be. Throughout my courses, I have learned about composition, the use of line, shape, form, value, texture, perspective, and color to create visual representations of concrete things and abstract concepts. I have also focused on learning about and using different materials to learn what I like best.

I have decided that my favorite material is either hand-cut paper, or ink pens. As you can see in my value and color projects for 2D design, I was able to create some interesting compositions with hand-cut paper, and I really liked that the finished pieces felt less fragil than the ones I made with chracoal or graphite. With the ink project, which is listed last on the page, I used my favorite ink pen: the Uniball Deluxe 0.7mm Roller Pen. I went through almost 4 of them on that project alone, but I learned that I really loved working with pens for the same reason I liked hand-cut paper: the permanance.
